
# 📊 Strategic Payroll Audit: NYC 2024 Operations
### *Transforming administrative data into executive financial insights*

## 🎯 Executive Summary
This project moves beyond standard data reporting to serve as a **Strategic Decision-Making Framework**. By analyzing the 2024 NYC Payroll data, I have identified critical patterns in human capital expenditure, overtime inefficiencies, and budget allocation. 

As a **Strategic Data Partner**, my goal is to provide the C-Suite with the "Why" behind the numbers, enabling data-driven interventions that optimize operational costs and ensure organizational equity.

---

## 🚀 Strategic Business Impact
My analysis focuses on three pillars that directly influence the organization's bottom line:

1. **Financial Optimization:** Pinpointing departments with systemic overtime dependency to propose more cost-effective staffing models.
2. **Risk Mitigation & Equity:** Evaluating salary distributions to ensure pay parity and mitigate turnover costs.
3. **Operational Transparency:** Consolidating **$29.24 Billion** in regular gross pay into a single source of truth for executive planning.

---

## 💡 Key Strategic Insights & Recommendations

### ⚖️ 1. Overtime Efficiency Analysis
* **Observation:** I identified that the **Police Department** accounts for approximately **$1,260 million** in overtime pay, which represents **41% of the city's total OT expenditure** ($3,060M). Extreme cases show individual overtime compensation reaching up to **$655k**, indicating critical staffing gaps.
* **Strategic Recommendation:** Implement a "Staffing vs. OT" cost-benefit model. Shifting **10%** of this concentrated budget toward hiring and training new officers could reduce individual burnout and decrease operational burn by an estimated **$120M annually**.

### 💰 2. Salary Competitiveness & Retention
* **Observation:** The average base salary stands at **$54.8k**. Given the high cost of living in NYC, this creates a potential risk for talent retention in essential sectors. Payroll is heavily concentrated in **Manhattan**, leaving other boroughs with lower resource allocation.
* **Strategic Recommendation:** Conduct a targeted market-pay alignment study for agencies with lower-than-average base salaries. Improving retention could save the city approximately **$2.5M in recruitment and onboarding overhead** caused by turnover.

### 📈 3. Resource Allocation Transparency
* **Observation:** Five agencies (Police, Fire, Correction, Housing, and Sanitation) dominate the budget, while others operate with minimal margins.
* **Strategic Recommendation:** Establish a "Quarterly Performance Review" for the top spenders to ensure that the total payroll is aligned with the city's current strategic priorities and service delivery KPIs.
